Inktober

Inktober prompts inspired the subject and medium for each of these drawings.

2022

Photo reference was found online. There are so many images to choose from. This one won me over because of the way that the wings flow from the gargoyle in a natural way, also beccause of the dramatic shadows and direct light on the gargoyle.

I started the sketch in pencil. Pencil is easier to correct while in the early stages, like getting the main parts in my drawing. Then went over it with a black ballpoint pen.

Sharpie and pen is all that is left in the final drawing. I use regular fine point black sharpie and a grey sharpie. Ballpoint pen, .7mm.

I added more texture and detail with the black ink pen.

The first time I sketched the scene was for the main idea and layout in pencil. The second time was more about the details, like limbs of characters and where the light / shadows would fall. The second detailed sketch was my reference for the final drawing.

The third light pencil sketch was drawn in my square sketchbook, where I create my final Inktober drawings. Then I went over the drawing with a black ballpoint ink pen. Letting the ink dry before removing the graphite from the surface of the page with a kneaded eraser.

At this point my drawing was mere line art. I continued shading with the one grey sharpie. This was to give the light direction and indicate dropped shadows.

Starting with the lighter of the two green shades in the 12 pack of alcohol based markers I use to ink in the color.

Then I use the lighter of the two oranges before the darker. It is easier to correct using this additive method.

Once I am satisfied with the general shapes drawn in pencil, the drawing is inked and the pencil marks are erased. This is the general outline of a bat flying through midair, drawn in ballpoint pen, black ink.

Then I take another pass at the drawing allowing myself to get lost in the details.

While I was using sharpies to layer the colors together, I was also paying close attention to warm and cool regions on the subject to help me decide where to put which colors. I like paying attention to warm and cool relationshps of color on living things or skin, because I like the effect it has on my art.

This decorative element was added to balance the composition, and also because I wanted to have fun using materials.

Original concept, no photo reference used, just imagination at play. I also wanted to draw simple cute skulls because that is a festive subject for inktober.

This stage is line art, created with a ballpoint ink pen. Just drawing while thinking about the form.

I wanted to draw them from multiple angles, trying to make it believable from the image that they belong together. I was testing my intuition for a sense of perspective.

With the line work out of the way, I am now free to consider colors or where the light is coming from.

For this medium, coffee was used at the end. I deviated from the inktober tradition, but I also excuse myself because the spirit and execution is mainly in ink. I don't have a tan or brown sharpie, I also like the natural texture.
